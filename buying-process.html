A practical checklist

Buying a lot starts with better questions.

Use RenoLeads to narrow your search, prepare for a conversation, and decide whether a site visit is the right next step.

Four steps

From first look to site visit.

1

Shortlist

Filter by property type, budget, or area. Save lots you want to compare later.

2

Review

Read the listing for price, lot size, location, and the information still worth verifying.

3

Ask

Request current photos, title and survey information, boundary details, access, and utility context.

4

Visit

Inspect the route and the land. Bring your questions and take notes you can compare.

Before you commit

  • Confirm ownership and document status with the appropriate professionals.
  • Inspect access, drainage, slope, utilities, and surrounding activity in person.
  • Ask how the lot boundaries and measurements were established.
  • Get final pricing and payment terms in writing before proceeding.
Site visit note

Listings are a starting point.

Photos and descriptions can help you decide what to inspect, but they cannot replace an on-site review or qualified advice. If a claim matters to your decision, ask how it can be verified.
